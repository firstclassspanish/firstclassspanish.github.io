Why is a book "el" but a table "la"? In Spanish, every noun has a gender.

masculino / femenino
every noun is one or the other

It's not about the object itself — it's just a category every Spanish noun belongs to. And once you know it, everything else agrees with it.

libro
el libro
usually -o = masculine
mesa
la mesa
usually -a = feminine

Most Spanish nouns follow a pattern — but not all. You'll learn to spot both.

libro
→
libros
papel
→
papeles

Gender is half the story. The other half? Making nouns plural — and Spanish has clear rules for that too.
