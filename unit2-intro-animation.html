Every sentence has a subject — the person or thing doing the action.

Every subject fits a category.

Every subject can be sorted by two things: whether it's 1st, 2nd, or 3rd person — and whether it's singular or plural, one or more than one.

In grammar, this is called person — and English already sorts pronouns into six boxes. The Box of Six.
