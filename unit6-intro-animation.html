Why does one of these sound wrong to a Spanish speaker's ear — even though every word is spelled correctly?

concordancia
(agreement)

In Spanish, articles and adjectives have to agree with the noun they describe — in gender and in number.

libro
→
el

Remember gender and number from Unit 5? Every noun still carries them — and now they ripple outward to everything around it.

el libro rojo (the red book)
la mesa roja (the red table)
los libros rojos (the red books)

Article, noun, adjective — all three match. Change the noun, and everything else shifts with it.
