# Unit 3 Concept Video — Script & Storyboard
*"Every Word Has a Job" — intro video for the Parts of Speech lesson*

**Length:** ~55 seconds
**Tone:** Curious, clean — this is a naming/labeling unit, not a heavy one. Keep it light.
**Style:** Talking-to-camera OR voiceover-over-animation both work with this script as written

---

## 0:00–0:08 — Hook

**VO:** "The dog runs fast. Every word in that sentence has a job."

**Visual:** The sentence "The dog runs fast." appears clean and simple, word by word.

---

## 0:08–0:20 — Bridge

**VO:** "Some words name things. Some show action. Some describe. Every word fits a category."

**Visual:** Each word in the sentence gets a small colored tag pop in beneath it: "The" → article, "dog" → noun, "runs" → verb, "fast" → adverb. Keep it simple — four tags, four colors, no explanation yet.

---

## 0:20–0:34 — Reveal

**VO:** "These categories are called parts of speech. And here's the useful part: they work the same way in Spanish."

**Visual:** The four category labels (article, noun, verb, adverb) settle into a clean list or small grid — calm, confident, "this is a real system" energy.

---

## 0:34–0:48 — Proof

**VO:** "El perro corre rápido. Same sentence, same categories, same logic."

**Visual:** The Spanish sentence appears with the exact same tag treatment: "El" → article, "perro" → noun, "corre" → verb, "rápido" → adverb — visually mirroring the English beat so the parallel is obvious without needing to explain it.

---

## 0:48–0:58 — Payoff

**VO:** "Once you know these categories, Spanish makes more sense — and so does English. It's also exactly what you'll need once we start conjugating verbs."

**Visual:** Quick, confident close — maybe the word "VERB" pulses or glows briefly, foreshadowing Unit 4 without dwelling on it.

---

## 0:58–1:03 — CTA

**VO:** "Let's meet the parts of speech."

**Visual:** Cut to Unit 3 title card.

**On-screen text:** Unit 3 · Parts of Speech

---

## Production notes

- **Keep this one short.** Unit 2's animation grew heavy through iteration — this script is intentionally lean from the start. Resist the urge to add more categories or more nuance to the video itself; anything beyond noun/verb/adjective/article/adverb belongs in the lesson, not here.
- **The English/Spanish mirroring is the whole trick.** Beat 2 and beat 4 should look visually identical except for the language — that parallel does more teaching than any explanation would.
- **No gender, no agreement, no conjugation details.** Those belong to future units (Concordancia, Conjugation). This video's only job is: words have categories, and the categories transfer across languages.
- **Same template as Units 1 and 2:** hook → bridge → reveal → proof → payoff → CTA. Keep using this for Unit 4 too, for consistency.
