# Unit 1 Concept Video — Script & Storyboard
*"Spanish Is Phonetic" — intro video for the Pronunciation lesson*

**Length:** ~65 seconds
**Tone:** Warm, direct, a little playful — like a teacher letting you in on something that'll make their life easier
**Style:** Talking-to-camera OR voiceover-over-animation both work with this script as written

---

## 0:00–0:07 — Hook

**VO:** "Quick question. Why does 'though,' 'through,' and 'tough' all end in the same four letters... but sound nothing alike?"

**Visual:** The three words appear on screen one at a time, large. Maybe a small confused-emoji beat, or just let the words sit — the absurdity does the work.

**On-screen text:** though / through / tough

---

## 0:07–0:18 — The problem, named

**VO:** "English pronunciation can be tricky to guess. Spanish is consistent — learn the rules once, and you're set."

**Visual:** The three English words scatter/shrink into the background, a little chaotic. Cut to clean, calm visual — maybe just the word "Spanish" settling into place.

---

## 0:18–0:32 — The reveal

**VO:** "Spanish is a phonetic language. That means every letter makes the same sound, every single time — no matter what word it's in. Learn the sound once, and it works everywhere."

**Visual:** Show the five vowels — a, e, i, o, u — appearing one at a time with their sound underneath (ah, eh, ee, oh, oo). Clean, confident, teal color family.

**On-screen text:** a → "ah" · e → "eh" · i → "ee" · o → "oh" · u → "oo"

---

## 0:32–0:48 — Prove it

**VO:** "Watch — here's a word you've probably never seen before."

**Visual:** The word **"escuela"** appears. Beat of silence — let it look intimidating for a second.

**VO (cont.):** "Break it into syllables, sound out each piece..."

**Visual:** The word splits into es · cue · la, each syllable highlighting in sequence, maybe with a soft tap sound per syllable (if using narration).

**VO (cont.):** "...and you can read it. Es-cue-la. School."

---

## 0:48–1:00 — The payoff

**VO:** "That's the whole idea behind Unit 1. Once you know the sounds, you're not memorizing word by word anymore — you can read almost anything."

**Visual:** A handful of other words flash briefly (gracias, hermano, amigo) as if to say "you've got this now" — quick, energetic, not lingering.

---

## 1:00–1:05 — CTA

**VO:** "Let's learn the sounds."

**Visual:** Cut to the Unit 1 lesson page / logo. Simple, confident close — no hard sell.

**On-screen text:** Unit 1 · Pronunciation

---

## Production notes

- **No forced ticket metaphor** — this video doesn't need it. The tagline already carries that; let this piece stand on its own.
- **Avoid Spain-specific pronunciation** — don't demonstrate the Castilian "th" sound for c/z. Keep it Latin American throughout.
- **Music:** something light and a little curious at the start, resolving to confident/upbeat by the payoff. Nothing that competes with narration.
- **If recording narration yourself:** keep pacing unhurried — this is for Spanish 1 students who may already feel behind. Confidence in your voice matters more than energy.
- **Reusability:** this same script structure (hook → problem → reveal → proof → payoff → CTA) can become the template for Unit 2–4 intros, swapping in each unit's core concept.
